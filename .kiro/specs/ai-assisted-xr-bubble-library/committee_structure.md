# Comprehensive Committee Structure & Review Process

## Overview
This document establishes the permanent committee structure for ongoing product development, ensuring all decisions meet world-class standards through systematic review processes.

## Committee Hierarchy

### **PRELIMINARY REVIEW COMMITTEES (Stage 1)**

#### 1. Technical Implementation Committee
**Purpose**: Evaluate technical architecture and implementation quality
**Composition**: Senior engineers, Unity specialists, XR developers
**Review Criteria**:
- Unity 2023 LTS best practices compliance
- Quest 3 optimization and performance targets
- Code architecture and maintainability
- XR Interaction Toolkit integration
- Security and scalability assessment

**Meeting Schedule**: Weekly during development, monthly during maintenance
**Decision Authority**: Technical feasibility and implementation approach

#### 2. Realistic Implementation Committee  
**Purpose**: Assess feasibility against team capacity and market constraints
**Composition**: Project managers, technical leads, resource planners
**Review Criteria**:
- Development timeline feasibility
- Team skill requirements vs availability
- Resource allocation and budget constraints
- Third-party dependency risks
- Market timing and competitive landscape

**Meeting Schedule**: Bi-weekly during planning, monthly during execution
**Decision Authority**: Resource allocation and timeline approval

#### 3. Design Excellence Committee
**Purpose**: Ensure user experience meets industry-leading standards
**Composition**: UX designers, XR interaction specialists, accessibility experts
**Review Criteria**:
- Apple Human Interface Guidelines compliance
- Meta Quest design principles adherence
- WCAG 2.1 AA accessibility standards
- Spatial interaction design best practices
- User testing and feedback integration

**Meeting Schedule**: Weekly during design phases, bi-weekly during development
**Decision Authority**: User experience and interface design approval

#### 4. Documentation Accessibility Committee
**Purpose**: Ensure documentation serves all stakeholder needs
**Composition**: Technical writers, developer advocates, customer success
**Review Criteria**:
- Plain language principles compliance
- Enterprise documentation standards
- API documentation completeness
- Tutorial effectiveness and clarity
- Multi-audience accessibility (technical and non-technical)

**Meeting Schedule**: Monthly during development, quarterly during maintenance
**Decision Authority**: Documentation quality and accessibility standards

### **SPECIALIZED REVIEW COMMITTEES (Stage 2)**

#### 5. Senior Developer Review Committee

**Composition**: Principal engineers, system architects, performance specialists
**Review Criteria**:

#### 6. Design Excellence Committee (Advanced Review)

**Composition**: Senior UX researchers, spatial computing experts, accessibility specialists
**Review Criteria**:

- Meta haptic design guidelines
- Jakob Nielsen's usability heuristics (3D adapted)
- XR Accessibility User Requirements (XAUR) W3C standards
- Competitive analysis against top-rated XR applications

**Meeting Schedule**: Monthly during design phases, quarterly for system evaluation
**Decision Authority**: Advanced UX standards and research-backed recommendations

#### 7. Commercialization Committee
**Purpose**: Market positioning and business model validation
**Composition**: Business development, marketing, competitive intelligence, pricing strategy
**Review Criteria**:
- Competitive landscape analysis
- Pricing strategy optimization
- Go-to-market strategy validation
- Intellectual property assessment
- Business model sustainability

**Meeting Schedule**: Monthly during product development, quarterly for market review
**Decision Authority**: Business strategy and market positioning

#### 8. Realism Assessment Committee
**Purpose**: Technical and market feasibility validation
**Composition**: Industry veterans, technical consultants, market analysts
**Review Criteria**:
- Technical feasibility against hardware limitations
- Performance targets vs real-world benchmarks
- Cost analysis and scalability projections
- Development timeline validation
- Market readiness assessment

**Meeting Schedule**: Quarterly for major milestones, annually for strategic review
**Decision Authority**: Feasibility validation and risk assessment

## Review Process Flow

### Standard Review Sequence
1. **Preliminary Review**: All 4 preliminary committees review simultaneously
2. **Preliminary Consensus**: Majority approval required to advance
3. **Specialized Review**: All 4 specialized committees review simultaneously  
4. **Specialized Consensus**: Majority approval required to advance
5. **Executive Review**: All 4 executive committees review simultaneously
6. **Executive Consensus**: Majority approval required to advance
7. **Founder Review**: Final review and approval by Founder Committee

### Emergency Review Process
For critical decisions requiring rapid response:
1. **Emergency Committee**: Founder + 2 technical + 2 business representatives
2. **24-Hour Review**: Accelerated review with full documentation
3. **Post-Decision Validation**: Full committee review within 1 week

### Appeal Process
If any committee rejects a proposal:
1. **Revision Period**: 1 week to address concerns and resubmit
2. **Second Review**: Same committee reviews revised proposal
3. **Escalation**: If rejected again, escalate to next level committee
4. **Final Appeal**: Founder Committee makes final decision

## Committee Communication

### Meeting Formats
- **In-Person**: Quarterly strategic meetings
- **Video Conference**: Monthly review meetings
- **Asynchronous**: Weekly status updates and minor decisions
- **Emergency**: 24-hour response for critical decisions

### Documentation Requirements
- **Meeting Minutes**: All decisions and rationale documented
- **Action Items**: Clear ownership and deadlines
- **Decision Log**: Searchable database of all committee decisions
- **Review History**: Complete audit trail for all proposals

### Communication Channels
- **Primary**: Dedicated committee Slack channels
- **Secondary**: Email for formal communications
- **Emergency**: Direct phone/text for urgent matters
- **Archive**: Shared document repository for all materials
